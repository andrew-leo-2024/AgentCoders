Applies coercive consequences (courts, regulators, sanctions).

Frankmax’s role:

Routes disputes to enforcement venues.

Preserves evidence integrity.

Freezes coordination state during enforcement.

Frankmax must never:

Punish

Decide guilt

Enforce outcomes

Frankmax hands off cleanly, then goes silent.

6. System of Evidence
What it does:
Defines what counts as proof.

Frankmax’s role:

Standardizes evidence formats across parties.

Separates human action from machine output.

Prevents narrative substitution for proof.

Frankmax must never:

Evaluate evidence

Judge credibility

Fill gaps

Frankmax makes evidence legible, not convincing.

7. System of Incentives
What it does:
Shapes behavior via rewards and penalties.

Frankmax’s role:

Applies negative incentives only: exclusion, decay, refusal.

Uses time and standing instead of rewards.

Frankmax must never:

Offer upside

Share profits

Optimize incentives

Frankmax pressures behavior without motivating it.

8. System of Trust
What it does:
Reduces friction via reputation and belief.

Frankmax’s role:

Replaces trust with defaults.

Makes trust unnecessary for coordination.

Frankmax must never:

Rate trust

Signal reliability

Build reputation narratives

Frankmax works best when nobody “trusts” it.

9. System of Meaning
What it does:
Explains why participation matters (values, mission).

Frankmax’s role:

Remains silent.

Allows others to project meaning if they want.

Frankmax must never:

Claim moral purpose

Use value language

Tell stories

Meaning creates allegiance.
Allegiance creates pressure.
Pressure breaks neutrality.

10. System of Boundary
What it does:
Defines where responsibility stops.

Frankmax’s role:

Explicitly declares non-responsibility zones.

Makes “we don’t do that” enforceable.

Frankmax must never:

Blur edges

Offer exceptions

Explain boundaries emotionally

Boundaries are mechanical or they don’t exist.

11. System of Irreversibility
What it does:
Creates point-of-no-return effects.

Frankmax’s role:

Makes protocol reference sticky.

Makes removal increase exposure.

Locks defaults once triggered.

Frankmax must never:

Allow quiet rollback

Restore standing without cost

Irreversibility is how standards become real.

12. System of Decay
What it does:
Prevents silent accumulation of power.

Frankmax’s role:

Expires standing.

Forces renewal.

Lets legitimacy rot if not maintained.

Frankmax must never:

Grant permanence

Preserve legacy privilege

Time is Frankmax’s strongest enforcement tool.

13. System of Silence
What it does:
Prevents narrative capture.

Frankmax’s role:

Codifies non-response as action.

Refuses to explain under pressure.

Frankmax must never:

Defend itself

Clarify misinterpretations

Engage media

Silence preserves non-agency.

14. System of Legibility to Power
What it does:
Allows regulators, courts, insurers to understand without steering.

Frankmax’s role:

Provides boring, reusable language.

Supplies safe analogies (standards, protocols).

Makes intervention look unnecessary.

Frankmax must never:

Be clever

Be opaque

Be ideological

Power attacks what it half-understands.

15. System of Human Discipline
What it does:
Prevents internal erosion.

Frankmax’s role:

Audits human actions.

Penalizes “helpfulness.”

Makes deviation visible and costly.

Frankmax must never:

Rely on virtue

Trust operators

Reward intelligence

Infrastructure survives dull custodians, not smart heroes.

The one-sentence synthesis (this is the click)
Frankmax is the neutral coordination grammar that lets all other systems operate together without collapsing into ownership, blame, or authority conflicts.

It does not compete with these systems.
It prevents them from killing each other.
